The Process
Four steps to knowing your risk
1

Answer 12 questions

Age, weight, height, family history, lifestyle habits. Takes under 5 minutes. No blood test. No login required. Available in Hindi.

Questions cover: Basic profile · Family history · Activity level · Diet · Sleep · Stress · Medical history
2

AI calculates your risk scores

Our ensemble ML model — benchmarked against 50M+ records from public health consortia (PIMA, UCI, UK Biobank) — processes your answers through 5 disease risk models in seconds.

Conditions analyzed: Diabetes · Heart disease · Hypertension · Metabolic risk · Sleep & stress risk
3

Get your personalized results

A clear dashboard showing your risk level for each condition (Low / Moderate / High), an overall health score, and plain-language explanations of why — not just what.

Results include: Risk score per condition · Overall health score · Top risk factors · Personalized recommendations
4

Act on your prevention plan

Specific, actionable steps tailored to your exact risk profile — nutrition changes, exercise targets, monitoring schedules, and when to see a doctor. Share your report with your physician.

Plan covers: Diet changes · Exercise protocol · Monitoring schedule · Doctor consultation triggers
The Science
How the AI actually works

Our risk models are informed by decades of epidemiological research — not invented by an algorithm. Here is what powers your score.

PIMA Indian Diabetes Dataset

768 female patients from the Pima Indian population. Key predictor of glucose, BMI, and hereditary diabetes risk factors in South Asian bodies.

UCI Heart Disease Dataset

303 patients across 76 attributes. Validated cardiovascular risk factors used in clinical research worldwide since 1988.

UK Biobank Population Study

500,000+ participants. Longitudinal study tracking lifestyle-to-disease outcomes over decades. Informs our 5-year risk horizon.

Important: PrediCare is an early-warning tool, not a medical diagnosis. Always consult a qualified physician for diagnosis and treatment. Our risk scores are educational indicators — not clinical assessments.
